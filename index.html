BIO

Having graduated from university with a Physics degree, I followed my passion for food and embarked on a successful career as a chef. Seven years later, after having reached a senior position in one of the best restaurants in the country, I realised that owning and running a restaurant wasn't what I wanted any more and decided upon a change.


My transition to the world of software was made with relative ease. I had some experience in C from university and, coupled with some web design skills picked up whilst creating my food blog, I had a good starting point. I did an internship at my brother's software company to level up on programming and web technologies.


I secured a job at FreeAgent as Support Engineer, a second-tier role providing technical support to both customers and the first-line support team, which was an ideal role in which to further my experience in programming, debugging and problem solving. Two years later I was promoted to a software engineering role in the Banking Team where I learned to work in a team of 5 - 6 engineers operating within an agile scrum-like framework.


With a wide range of programming experience under my belt in a relatively short space of time, I'm now ready to broaden my experience further and also put the skills I've learned to effective use. Ideally I'd like to find an engineering position in the food industry, which would combine my technical expertise with my domain knowledge in gastronomy.
